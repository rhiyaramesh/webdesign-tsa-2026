Events of Virginia Beach

Explore the events that have shaped the city of Virginia Beach.

First Landing in Virginia

At 4:00 AM on April 26, 1607, three ships carrying 104 colonists arrived at Cape Henry, near modern-day Virginia Beach. These included Susan Constant, Godspeed, and Discovery, under the command of Captain Christopher Newport. These colonists established the first English colony in the “New World” which would lead to the rise of America.

Battle of the Capes

The Battle of the Capes, fought on September 5, 1781, near Chesapeake Bay, was a crucial naval battle in the American Revolutionary War. The French fleet's victory blocked British reinforcements from reaching Yorktown, allowing George Washington and his allies to lay siege to British forces and secure American independence.

The Maple Leaf Escape

On June 10, 1863, nearly 70 Confederate officers escaped from the Union steamer Maple Leaf during the American Civil War. This occurred during the transport to Fort Delaware, overpowering their captors off the Virginia coast. The prisoners, originally bound for a prisoner exchange, staged a daring revolt, successfully landing near False Cape, North Carolina.

Neptune's Festival

In 1973, Richard Kline, desired to create an event that would bring the city together, attract the interest of tourists and celebrate the heritage of the city. There were 50,000 people in attendance at the first festival and its legacy only grew from there.
