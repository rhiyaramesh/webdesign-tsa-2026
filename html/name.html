Origins of the Name

How Virginia Beach got its name and how that identity evolved over time.

The Story Behind the Name

From Cape Henry, to Princess Anne County, to Virginia Beach, the area’s identity changed from early exploration landmarks to the modern name known today.

Cape Henry

On April 26, 1607, the first permanent English settlers from the London Company set up a wooden cross on the newly named Cape Henry shore in gratitude to God and to affirm England’s claim to the site. Also, when Captain John Smith’s party returned there, they erected a cross and named the spot Cape Henry, after the Prince of Wales.

Princess Anne County

In 1951, Mrs. Rebecca Cooke and Mrs. Lillian Robertson initiated the formation of a Daughters of the American Revolution chapter in Virginia Beach. By mid-year, 14 potential members were identified, and Princess Anne County was selected as the chapter name, honoring Princess Anne, daughter of King James II and future Queen of England.

Virginia Beach

It is not clearly recorded at what exact time it was named Virginia Beach, but in 1883, the name “Virginia Beach” was used. Later, parts of Princess Anne County were merged with Virginia Beach, creating Virginia Beach City.
