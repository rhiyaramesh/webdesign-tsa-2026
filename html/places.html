Historical Places

Important places help tell the story of Virginia Beach and its development over time.

First Landing State Park

First Landing State Park marks the site where English settlers first arrived in 1607. Today, it is a protected natural area where visitors can explore trails, beaches, and historical markers that connect them to one of the earliest moments in American history.

False Cape State Park

False Cape received its name because its land mass resembled Cape Henry at Virginia Beach, luring boats into shallow waters in the 1800s. One of the area’s first communities, Wash Woods, was developed by survivors of such a shipwreck. Today, the remains of Wash Woods include a cemetery and a church steeple, giving visitors a glimpse into history while enjoying nature.

Neptune's Park

Named after the iconic 34-foot bronze statue of King Neptune, a Roman god of the sea, Neptune’s Park is a popular beachfront destination that serves as a central attraction. Being the host location of Neptune’s festival, the park is a destination of happenings for residents of Virginia Beach, it's also a place that brings people together, with a history of connection originating from the Neptune’s festival in 1973.
